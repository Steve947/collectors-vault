Privacy Policy

Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 20 July 2026  ·  Effective 20 July 2026

Hoardr is a collection manager for hobbyist collectors. This policy explains what we store, where it lives, who else touches it, and what we will never do with it.

The short version
  • We store your email address and whatever you choose to catalogue.
  • We do not sell or share your personal information, show advertising, or run analytics or tracking of any kind.
  • Your collection is readable only by your own account.
  • You can export everything, or delete your account and all its data, from inside the app at any time.

1. Who we are

Hoardr ("we", "us") is a collection-management application operated by an independent developer based in Australia. Hoardr is the data controller for the personal information described in this policy.

Contact: privacy@hoardrapp.com for privacy matters, support@hoardrapp.com for everything else. We aim to respond to privacy requests within 30 days.

2. What we collect

Account information

Your email address. If you sign in with Google we also receive your display name and profile picture URL where you have one. Sign-in is handled by Google Firebase Authentication; if you use an email and password, that password is stored and verified by Firebase and is never visible to us.

Your collection content

Everything you choose to enter: item names, descriptions, categories, groups, condition, materials, dimensions, dates, purchase prices, estimated values, sellers, provenance, certificate and serial references, insurance and storage notes, tags, ratings, and photographs.

Some of this can be sensitive — storage locations and valuations especially. We treat all of it as private. It is readable only by your own account.

Preferences

Your display currency, and whether you have completed onboarding or dismissed the install prompt.

Technical and security information

Firebase records operational data including sign-in timestamps and IP address, used for security and abuse prevention. We use Google reCAPTCHA v3 through Firebase App Check to confirm requests come from the genuine Hoardr app rather than an automated script. reCAPTCHA analyses device and interaction signals and is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

Hoardr does not use advertising or tracking cookies. It does store data on your own device so the app works offline and remembers your settings:

5. Where your data is stored

Hoardr runs on Google Firebase. Collection records are held in Google Cloud Firestore and photographs in Google Cloud Storage in the United States (region us-east1).

If you are outside the United States — including in Australia, the UK or the EEA — your personal information is transferred to and stored in the United States. Google LLC provides these services under its own data-protection terms, which include the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses for transfers out of the EEA and UK. See Firebase's privacy and security information.

8. Your rights and controls

Export everything

Save to Device produces a complete JSON file of your collections, groups, items and photographs — readable without Hoardr. This satisfies your right to data portability.

Delete everything

Profile → Delete my account & all data permanently removes your account, every collection, group and item, and every photograph in storage. It is immediate and cannot be undone, so export first if you want a copy.

Access and correction

Everything we hold about you is visible and editable in the app. For a copy in another format, or any question about your data, email privacy@hoardrapp.com.

Other rights

Depending on where you live you may also have the right to object to or restrict processing, withdraw consent, and lodge a complaint with a regulator. We will not discriminate against you for exercising any privacy right.

9. Regional rights

Australia

We handle personal information in line with the Australian Privacy Principles under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth). If you are unhappy with our response to a privacy complaint you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner at oaic.gov.au.

UK and EEA

You have rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability and objection under the UK and EU GDPR, and the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

California

Under the CCPA/CPRA you have the right to know what personal information is collected, to delete it, to correct it, and to opt out of sale or sharing. We do not sell or share personal information, so there is nothing to opt out of. The categories we collect are identifiers (email address), internet activity (security logs), and user content (your collection and photographs), as described in section 2.

10. Retention

Your data is kept for as long as your account exists. When you delete your account it is removed from the live database and storage immediately. Residual copies may persist briefly in Google's routine backups before being overwritten. Support emails are kept while needed to resolve your enquiry.

11. Security and breach notification

Access is enforced by owner-only security rules on both the database and photo storage, traffic is encrypted in transit with HTTPS/TLS, and Firebase App Check is enforced to reject requests that do not come from the genuine app. Uploads are limited to images under 5 MB.

No system is perfectly secure. If a breach occurs that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify affected users and the relevant regulator as required by law — including the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme in Australia and Articles 33–34 of the GDPR.

12. Children

Hoardr is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect their personal information. If you believe a child has created an account, contact us and we will delete it.

13. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially we will update the version and date at the top of this page and, where the change is significant, notify you in the app before it takes effect.
